3 Areas where I shaped AI work

Content Strategy for Agentic Systems

What is it?

Aampe needed a way to see content as a living portfolio, not a backlog of messages. The content map connected goals, topics, contexts, brand inputs, and agent decisions.

What was done?

  • Created the content map framework and prototype with Strategy, GTM, and Leadership.
  • Turned scattered inputs into a bird's-eye view teams could use to spot gaps, overlaps, and next bets.
  • Designed it as structured context for agents, so planning could feed creation, observation, and improvement.

Label Frameworks for Semantic Strategy

What is it?

Labels were not just tags. They were the meaning layer that helped brands define what to say, how it should vary, and how AI should judge relevance.

What was done?

  • Built a framework for labels around intent, value propositions, tone, context, and decision moments.
  • Moved teams from ad hoc taxonomy work to a semantic strategy brands could understand and extend.
  • Made labels usable across generation, validation, analysis, and agent learning loops.

Content Generation at Scale

What is it?

As generation expanded to thousands of copy lines, the bottleneck shifted from writing to orchestration and QA. Teams needed fast tools to generate, modify, review, and validate content safely.

What was done?

  • Shaped and vibe-coded AI tools like Relay and Label Buddy to accelerate production.
  • Designed flows for components, context profiles, modifiers, semantic checks, and review queues.
  • Turned manual strategy work into repeatable systems teams could test with customers live.
